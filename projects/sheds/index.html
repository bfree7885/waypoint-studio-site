Sheds

A terrain-based field system for understanding shed antler distribution

What It Is

Sheds is a map-first application designed to help users understand where naturally shed antlers are likely to be found by analyzing terrain, habitat, and real-world observations.

Rather than relying on anecdotal knowledge or random search, Sheds approaches shed hunting as a structured environmental problem—one shaped by slope, aspect, bedding behavior, thermal patterns, and seasonal movement.

The system is built around a simple idea:

Location is not random. It is patterned.

Sheds exists to make those patterns visible.

Core System

Terrain-Based Probability Mapping

Sheds models the landscape using key terrain variables known to influence deer behavior:

  • Slope (movement cost and bedding preference)
  • Aspect (solar exposure and thermal regulation)
  • Terrain position (ridges, benches, transitions)
  • Habitat context (edge, cover, pressure zones)

These variables are combined to generate probability-weighted search zones rather than fixed “hotspots.”

The goal is not to tell users exactly where to go—but to help them understand why certain areas are more likely than others.

Field View (Map-First Interface)

The primary interface is a live, map-based environment where users can:

  • Explore terrain layers
  • Interpret slope and aspect in real time
  • Move through landscapes with context, not guesswork

Sheds is designed to be used in the field, not just at a desk.

Ethical System Design

Sheds enforces constraints at the system level to reduce negative impact on wildlife:

  • No hotspot visualization during closed seasons
  • Automatic transition to an education-only mode when appropriate
  • Emphasis on terrain understanding rather than location exploitation

This is not a shortcut tool.
It is a learning system.

Data System

Sheds is built to generate a structured dataset from real-world observations.

Each user-submitted shed becomes a data point.

Extracted From Photos:

  • Beam length (estimated)
  • Point count
  • Condition (fresh, weathered, damaged)
  • Estimated age class

Paired With Terrain Data:

  • Slope
  • Aspect
  • Elevation
  • Distance to water
  • Habitat type

Over time, this creates a growing dataset linking physical antler characteristics to specific terrain conditions.

Why This Matters

Shed hunting is typically treated as a recreational activity with informal knowledge.

Sheds treats it differently:

As a distributed field data collection system.

Each observation contributes to understanding:

  • Where animals move under late-season conditions
  • How terrain influences energy conservation
  • How bedding and travel patterns manifest spatially

This shifts the activity from isolated experience to collective environmental insight.

Long-Term Direction

Sheds is not an endpoint. It is an entry point into a broader system.

The long-term goals are:

  • Build a structured dataset of shed ecology
  • Identify repeatable terrain-driven patterns
  • Support research into wildlife movement and habitat use
  • Contribute to larger environmental and geospatial intelligence systems

Over time, this system can extend beyond shed hunting into:

  • Habitat modeling
  • Movement prediction
  • Landscape-level analysis of animal behavior

Position

Sheds is not:

  • A shortcut to success
  • A guaranteed finder
  • A gamified hunting tool

Sheds is:

  • A terrain interpretation system
  • A field-based data engine
  • A long-term environmental mapping project

Closing

Understanding land requires time, observation, and pattern recognition.

Sheds is designed to support that process—not replace it.

Focused on long-term development of geospatial and resilience systems.
